## Getting Started

### Opening a Document

When you click on a document to view it, the PDF viewer will open in full-screen mode. The document will load automatically, and you'll see a progress indicator while it loads.

**What you'll see:**
- Document title at the top
- Navigation controls in the toolbar
- The PDF content in the main viewing area
- Zoom controls on the right side of the toolbar

### First-Time Loading

The first time you open a document, it may take a few seconds to load depending on:
- Your internet connection speed
- The size of the PDF file
- The number of pages in the document

**Tip:** Once loaded, pages are cached for faster viewing next time!

## Viewing Documents

### View Modes

The PDF viewer supports two viewing modes:

#### Single Page Mode (Default)
- Shows one page at a time
- Use navigation buttons or keyboard to move between pages
- Best for focused reading

#### Continuous Scroll Mode
- Shows all pages vertically
- Scroll naturally through the document
- Best for long documents or quick browsing

**Note:** The view mode is automatically saved and will be remembered for your next session.

### Page Display

Each page is rendered clearly and can be zoomed for better readability. The viewer automatically adjusts to your screen size for optimal viewing.

## Zoom and View Controls

### Zoom Levels

The viewer supports zoom levels from 50% to 300%:

- **50%** - Smallest view (see more of the page)
- **100%** - Normal size (default)
- **300%** - Largest view (see fine details)

### Zoom Controls

**Zoom In Button** (üîç+)
- Click to increase zoom by 25%
- Disabled at maximum zoom (300%)

**Zoom Out Button** (üîç-)
- Click to decrease zoom by 25%
- Disabled at minimum zoom (50%)

**Zoom Level Display**
- Shows current zoom percentage
- Located between zoom buttons

### Zoom Tips

1. **Use Ctrl+Scroll** for quick zooming:
   - Scroll up while holding Ctrl = Zoom in
   - Scroll down while holding Ctrl = Zoom out

2. **Zoom maintains your position:**
   - The viewer keeps you centered on the same content
   - No need to scroll back to where you were

3. **Zoom works in both view modes:**
   - Single page mode: Zooms the current page
   - Continuous scroll mode: Zooms all pages

### Touch Navigation

**Swipe Left**

**Pinch Out (spread fingers)**

## Troubleshooting

### Document Won't Load

**Problem:** The document shows a loading spinner but never loads.

**Solutions:**
1. **Check your internet connection:**
   - Ensure you're connected to the internet
   - Try refreshing the page

2. **Wait a bit longer:**
   - Large documents may take time to load
   - Look for the progress percentage

3. **Try again later:**
   - The document link may have expired
   - Contact the document owner for a new link

### Pages Are Blank

**Problem:** The viewer loads but pages appear blank.

**Solutions:**
1. **Wait for rendering:**
   - Pages may still be rendering
   - Look for a "Rendering..." message

2. **Try zooming:**
   - Sometimes zoom level affects rendering
   - Try zooming in or out

3. **Refresh the page:**
   - Close and reopen the document
   - This clears the cache and starts fresh

### Slow Performance

**Problem:** The viewer is slow or laggy.

**Solutions:**
1. **Close other tabs:**
   - Free up browser memory
   - Close unnecessary applications

2. **Use continuous scroll mode:**
   - Better for long documents
   - More efficient memory usage

3. **Reduce zoom level:**
   - Lower zoom = faster rendering
   - Try 100% or 75% zoom

### Zoom Not Working

**Problem:** Zoom buttons are disabled or don't work.

**Solutions:**
1. **Check zoom limits:**
   - Can't zoom below 50%
   - Can't zoom above 300%

2. **Try keyboard shortcuts:**
   - Use Ctrl+Scroll instead
   - May work when buttons don't

3. **Refresh the page:**
   - Restart the viewer
   - Should reset zoom controls

### Navigation Buttons Disabled

**Problem:** Previous or Next buttons are grayed out.

**Reasons:**
- **Previous disabled:** You're on the first page
- **Next disabled:** You're on the last page
- **Both disabled:** Document has only one page

**This is normal behavior!** The buttons are disabled when you can't navigate in that direction.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I download the PDF?

The download option depends on the document owner's settings. Some documents may have download restrictions for security reasons.

### Can I print the document?

Printing may be disabled for certain documents to protect sensitive information. If you need a printed copy, contact the document owner.

### Why can't I select text?

Text selection may be disabled for documents with DRM (Digital Rights Management) protections. This prevents unauthorized copying of content.

### How long does the document link last?

Document links typically expire after a certain time for security. If your link has expired, request a new one from the document owner.

### Can I view documents offline?

No, documents must be loaded from the server each time. An internet connection is required to view documents.

### Why do I see a watermark?

Watermarks are added by document owners to:
- Identify who is viewing the document
- Prevent unauthorized distribution
- Track document usage

Watermarks are a normal part of document security and cannot be removed.

### What browsers are supported?

The PDF viewer works on all modern browsers:
- ‚úÖ Google Chrome (recommended)
- ‚úÖ Mozilla Firefox
- ‚úÖ Safari
- ‚úÖ Microsoft Edge

**Note:** For the best experience, use the latest version of your browser.

### Does it work on mobile devices?

Yes! The PDF viewer is fully responsive and works on:
- üì± Smartphones (iOS and Android)
- üì± Tablets (iPad, Android tablets)
- üíª Desktop computers
- üíª Laptops

### How do I report a problem?

If you encounter issues:
1. Note what you were doing when the problem occurred
2. Take a screenshot if possible
3. Contact support with:
   - Document name or ID
   - Browser and device information
   - Description of the problem

### Can I view multiple documents at once?

You can open multiple documents in separate browser tabs. Each document will have its own viewer instance.

### Why is the document loading slowly?

Loading speed depends on:
- **Document size:** Larger PDFs take longer
- **Number of pages:** More pages = more loading time
- **Internet speed:** Faster connection = faster loading
- **Server load:** High traffic may slow things down

**Tip:** Once loaded, pages are cached for faster viewing!

### What if I close the viewer accidentally?

Your reading position is automatically saved. When you reopen the document, you'll return to the last page you were viewing.

### Can I adjust the brightness or contrast?

The viewer displays documents as they were created. Brightness and contrast adjustments are not currently available. You can adjust your device's screen brightness instead.

### Is my viewing activity tracked?

Document owners may track:
- When you view a document
- Which pages you view
- How long you spend viewing

This helps document owners understand how their content is being used.

### Can I share the document with others?

Sharing depends on the document owner's settings. Some documents can be shared, while others are restricted to specific users.

### What happens if my internet disconnects?

If your connection drops while viewing:
- Already loaded pages will remain visible
- New pages won't load until connection is restored
- Refresh the page once your connection is back
